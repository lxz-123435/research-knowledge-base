[**Everett M. Rogers**](chatgpt://generic-entity?number=0 "Everett M. Rogers")（1931–2004）（埃弗雷特·罗杰斯）是20世纪最具影响力的传播学与创新研究学者之一，美国传播学家与社会学家，以提出并系统化 \*\*“创新扩散理论（Diffusion of Innovations）”**而闻名，是理解**技术如何从“被发明”走向“被采用”的经典权威人物。Rogers 出生于美国爱荷华州，早年接受农村社会学训练，后转向传播学研究，长期任教于斯坦福大学、南加州大学等高校。他的研究横跨**传播学、社会学、发展研究与公共政策**，特别关注新技术、新制度在不同社会情境中的扩散过程。

### **1. 创新扩散理论（Diffusion of Innovations）**

Rogers 提出，创新的社会扩散并非自然或自动发生，而是一个**受传播结构、社会关系与个体认知共同影响的过程**。其核心要点包括：

- **创新五大属性**：
  - 相对优势（relative advantage）
  - 相容性（compatibility）
  - 复杂性（complexity）
  - 可试验性（trialability）
  - 可观察性（observability）
    这些属性系统性地影响创新被接受的速度与广度。
- **采用者类型五分法**：
  - 创新者（innovators）
  - 早期采用者（early adopters）
  - 早期大众（early majority）
  - 晚期大众（late majority）
  - 落后者（laggards）
    该分类揭示了技术扩散中的**社会分层与路径依赖**。

### **2. “传播渠道—时间—社会系统”框架**

Rogers 强调，创新扩散是一个包含四个关键要素的系统过程：

> **创新本身 × 传播渠道 × 时间 × 社会系统**

这使得技术扩散研究从“技术性能”分析，拓展为对**制度环境、社会结构和传播网络**的综合考察。

### **3. 从“供给视角”到“采用视角”的转变**

与以发明者和技术供给为中心的传统研究不同，Rogers 将分析重心转向**使用者与社会接受机制**，揭示了大量“好技术但难以推广”的现实原因，为政策设计和技术推广提供了重要启示。

Diffusion is the process by which an innovation is communicated through certain channels over time among the members of a social system. Diffusion is a special type of communication concerned with the spread of messages that are perceived as new ideas. Communication is a process in which participants create and share information with one another in order to reach a mutual understanding. Diffusion has a special character because of the newness of the idea in the message content. Thus some degree of uncertainty and perceived risk is involved in the diffusion process. An individual can reduce this degree of uncertainty by obtaining information. Information is a difference in matter energy that affects uncertainty in a situation where a choice exists among a set of alternatives. &#x20;
The main elements in the diffusion of new ideas are: (1) an innovation (2) that is communicated through certain channels (3) over time (4) among the members of a social system.

技术扩散是指一项创新通过特定渠道，随时间在某一社会系统成员间传播的过程。技术扩散是一种特殊的传播形式，核心在于传递被视为新想法的信息。传播则是参与者之间创造并共享信息，以达成相互理解的过程。由于信息内容中蕴含的想法具有新颖性，技术扩散具备了独特属性 —— 因此，这一过程必然涉及一定程度的不确定性与感知风险。个体可通过获取信息来降低这种不确定性。信息是物质能量的差异，在存在多种选择的情境中，这种差异会影响不确定性的程度。

We often use the word "innovation" and "technology" as synonyms. A technology is a design for instrumental action that reduces the uncertainty in the cause-effect relationships involved in achieving a desired outcome. A technology usually has two components: (1) a hardware aspect, consisting of the tool that embodies the technology as a material or physical object, and (2) a software aspect, consisting of the information base for the tool. For example, we often speak of (1) "computer hardware," consisting of semiconductors, transistors, electrical connections, and a frame that protects these electronic components, and (2) "computer software," consisting of the coded commands, instructions, manuals, and other information aspects of this tool that allow us to use it for certain tasks. This example illustrates the close relationship between hardware and software, between a tool and the way it is used. &#x20;
We often think of technology mainly in terms of hardware. Indeed, sometimes the hardware side of a technology is dominant. But in other cases, a technology may be almost entirely composed of information; examples are a political philosophy such as Marxism, a religious idea such as Christianity, a news event, and a policy such as a municipal no-smoking ordinance. The diffusion of such software innovations has been investigated, although a methodological problem in such studies is that their adoption cannot be so easily traced or observed. Such idea-only innovations have a relatively lower degree of observability and thus a slower rate of adoption. &#x20;
A number of new products involve both a hardware component and a software component, with the hardware purchased first so that the software component can then be utilized. Examples are VCRs and videotapes, compact disc players and CDs, and personal computers and computer software programs. Often a company will sell a hardware product at a relatively low price in order to capture market share and then sell its software at a relatively high price in order to recover profits. An example is video game players; these are sold at a fairly low price, but the video games to be played on them are sold at a relatively high price. This "shaver-and-blades" strategy is commonly used to speed the diffusion of consumer electronics innovations.

我们常常将 “创新”（innovation）与 “技术”（technology）视为同义词。技术是一种工具性行动的设计，其核心作用是降低实现预期结果过程中因果关系的不确定性。一项技术通常包含两个组成部分：（1）硬件层面，即作为物质或物理实体承载技术的工具；（2）软件层面，即该工具的信息基础。例如，我们常说的（1）“计算机硬件”，由半导体、晶体管、电路连接部件及保护这些电子元件的外壳构成；（2）“计算机软件”，则包含编码指令、操作指南、使用手册及该工具其他用于完成特定任务的信息类要素。这一例子清晰体现了硬件与软件、工具与其使用方式之间的紧密关联。

我们往往主要从硬件角度理解技术。事实上，有时技术的硬件层面确实占据主导地位。但在其他情况下，一项技术可能几乎完全由信息构成；例如，马克思主义这样的政治哲学、基督教这样的宗教思想、某一新闻事件，以及城市禁烟令这类政策。此类软件类创新的扩散已得到研究，但这类研究存在一个方法论难题：其采用过程难以被清晰追踪或观察。这类纯理念型创新的可观测性相对较低，因此其采用速度也更为缓慢。

许多新产品同时包含硬件和软件两个组成部分，通常需先购买硬件，才能使用相应的软件。例如，录像机（VCRs）与录像带、激光唱机与激光唱片（CDs），以及个人电脑与计算机软件程序。企业往往会以相对较低的价格销售硬件产品以抢占市场份额，随后通过以较高价格销售软件来收回利润。游戏机便是典型例子：游戏机本身售价相当低廉，但可在其上运行的游戏软件售价则相对较高。这种 “剃须刀 - 刀片”（shaver-and-blades）策略常被用于加速消费电子类创新的扩散。

# Four Main Elements in the Diffusion of Innovations

Previously we defined diffusion as the process by which (1) an innovation (2) is communicated through certain channels (3) over time (4) among the members of a social system. The four main elements are the innovation, communication channels, time, and the social system (Figure 1–2). These elements are identifiable in every diffusion research study and in every diffusion campaign or program (such as the attempted diffusion of water boiling in a Peruvian village).

# 创新扩散的四大核心要素

此前，我们将创新扩散定义为这样一个过程：**（1）一项创新，（2）通过特定渠道，（3）历经一定时间，（4）在某一社会系统的成员之间传播**。这四大核心要素分别是创新本身、传播渠道、时间以及社会系统（见图 1-2）。无论是在每一项创新扩散的学术研究中，还是在每一次创新扩散的实践活动或推广项目中（例如在秘鲁某村庄推广饮水煮沸消毒的尝试），都能识别出这四类要素的存在。
