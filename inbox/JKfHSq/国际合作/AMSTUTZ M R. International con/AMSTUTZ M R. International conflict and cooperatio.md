Joseph Grieco defines international cooperation “as the voluntary adjustment by
states of their policies so that they manage their differences and reach some mutu-
ally beneficial outcome.”4 In global politics, international cooperation is normally
characterized by three features: (1) the actions of states (and other nonstate actors)
are voluntary; (2) cooperation involves the identification of, and commitment to,
shared goals; and (3) cooperation results in beneficial outcomes to the participants.
When cooperation is regularized and establishes patterns of behavior, it results in
an international regime, a concept examined later in this chapter.
