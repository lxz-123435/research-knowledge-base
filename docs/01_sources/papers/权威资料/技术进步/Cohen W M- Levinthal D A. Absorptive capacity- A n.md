### **二、核心理论贡献**

1. **吸收能力理论（Absorptive Capacity）**

   在经典论文*Absorptive Capacity: A New Perspective on Learning and Innovation*（1989/1990）中，Cohen 与 Levinthal 提出：
   > 企业（或国家）从外部获取技术溢出收益的能力，并非自动发生，而取决于其**识别、吸收与利用外部知识的能力**。
   > 吸收能力的核心来源包括：
   - 既有知识存量与研发投入；
   - 组织学习机制与人力资本结构；
   - 跨组织、跨网络的互动经验。
     该理论首次在机制层面解释了 **“为什么面对同样的外部知识环境，不同主体的创新绩效差异巨大”**。
2. **知识外溢的“内生化”解释**

   相较于将知识外溢视为纯粹外生公共品的传统观点（如 Arrow, 1962），Cohen 强调：
   - 知识外溢的实际效应高度依赖接收方能力；
   - 研发投入不仅用于“创造新知识”，也用于“学习已有知识”；
   - 因此，创新收益具有明显的**能力门槛与路径依赖特征**。
3. **企业研发的双重功能**

   Cohen 提出，研发活动同时承担两种功能：
   - **创新功能**（直接产出新技术）；
   - **学习功能**（提升吸收能力）。
     这一观点极大拓展了对研发投资回报的理解，被广泛应用于企业创新战略、产业政策和技术合作研究。

# Introduction

In this paper, we argue that the ability of a firm to recognize the value of new, external information, assimilate it, and apply it to commercial ends is critical to its innovative capabilities. We label this capability a firm's absorptive capacity and suggest that it is largely a function of the firm's level of prior related knowledge. The discussion focuses first on the cognitive basis for an individual's absorptive capacity including, in particular, prior related knowledge and diversity of background. We then characterize the factors that influence absorptive capacity at the organizational level, how an organization's absorptive capacity differs from that of its individual members, and the role of diversity of expertise within an organization. We argue that the development of absorptive capacity, and, in turn, innovative performance are history- or path-dependent and argue how lack of investment in an area of expertise early on may foreclose the future development of a technical capability in that area. We formulate a model of firm investment in research and development (R\&D), in which R\&D contributes to a firm's absorptive capacity, and test predictions relating a firm's investment in R\&D to the knowledge underlying technical change within an industry. Discussion focuses on the implications of absorptive capacity for the analysis of other related innovative activities, including basic research, the adoption and diffusion of innovations, and decisions to participate in cooperative R\&D ventures.

Outside sources of knowledge are often critical to the innovation process, whatever the organizational level at which the innovating unit is defined. While the example of Japan illustrates the point saliently at the national level (e.g., Westney and Sakakibara, 1986; Mansfield, 1988; Rosenberg and Steinmueller, 1988), it is also true of entire industries, as pointed out by Brock (1975) in the case of computers and by Peck (1962) in the case of aluminum. At the organizational level, March and Simon (1958: 188) suggested most innovations result from borrowing rather than invention. This observation is supported by extensive research on the sources of innovation (e.g., Mueller, 1962; Hamberg, 1963; Myers and Marquis, 1969; Johnston and Gibbons, 1975; von Hippel, 1988). Finally, the importance to innovative performance of information originating from other internal units in the firm, outside the formal innovating unit (i.e., the R\&D lab), such as marketing and manufacturing, is well understood (e.g., Mansfield, 1968). &#x20;

The ability to exploit external knowledge is thus a critical component of innovative capabilities. We argue that the ability to evaluate and utilize outside knowledge is largely a function of the level of prior related knowledge. At the most elemental level, this prior knowledge includes basic skills or even a shared language but may also include knowledge of the most recent scientific or technological developments in a given field. Thus, prior related knowledge confers an ability to recognize the value of new information, assimilate it, and apply it to commercial ends. These abilities collectively constitute what we call a firm's "absorptive capacity."

# &#x20;引言 &#x20;

本文认为，企业**识别新外部信息价值、消化吸收该信息并将其应用于商业用途**的能力，是其创新能力的核心所在。我们将这种能力界定为**企业吸收能力**，并提出该能力在很大程度上取决于企业已有的相关知识储备水平。

本文的论述首先聚焦于个体吸收能力的认知基础，其中特别强调了**先验相关知识**与**背景多样性**两大要素。随后，我们进一步阐释了影响组织层面吸收能力的各类因素，分析了组织吸收能力与组织内个体吸收能力的差异，并探讨了组织内部专业技能多样性的作用。

本文提出，企业吸收能力的培育乃至创新绩效的提升，均具有**历史依赖性或路径依赖性**；同时指出，若企业早期在某一专业领域投入不足，可能会阻碍该领域技术能力的后续发展。基于此，我们构建了企业研发（R\&D）投资模型，在该模型中，研发投入不仅能推动企业创新，更有助于提升企业吸收能力；并通过实证检验了企业研发投入与行业技术变革底层知识之间的关联性假设。

最后，本文重点论述了吸收能力理论对其他相关创新活动分析的启示意义，研究范畴涵盖基础研究、创新的采纳与扩散，以及企业参与合作研发项目的决策机制等方面。

无论创新单元的组织层面如何定义，外部知识来源对创新过程往往至关重要。日本的案例在国家层面显著印证了这一点（例如Westney & Sakakibara, 1986；Mansfield, 1988；Rosenberg & Steinmueller, 1988）；而在行业层面，这一结论同样成立——Brock（1975）针对计算机行业、Peck（1962）针对铝行业的研究均指出了外部知识的关键作用。在组织层面，March与Simon（1958: 188）提出，大多数创新源于“借鉴”而非“发明”，这一观点得到了大量关于创新来源的研究支持（如Mueller, 1962；Hamberg, 1963；Myers & Marquis, 1969；Johnston & Gibbons, 1975；von Hippel, 1988）。此外，来自企业内部非正规创新单元（如研发实验室之外的营销、制造部门）的信息，对创新绩效的重要性也已得到充分认可（如Mansfield, 1968）。 &#x20;

因此，利用外部知识的能力是创新能力的关键组成部分。本文认为，评估和利用外部知识的能力，在很大程度上取决于企业“先前相关知识”的积累水平。从最基础的层面来看，这类先前知识包括基本技能、共享语言，也可能涵盖特定领域最新的科技发展成果。正是凭借先前相关知识，企业才能够识别新信息的价值、吸收新信息，并将其应用于商业目标。这些能力共同构成了我们所说的企业“吸收能力”（absorptive capacity）。
