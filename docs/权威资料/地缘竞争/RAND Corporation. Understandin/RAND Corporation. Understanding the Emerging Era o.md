“For the purposes of this project, we conceive of order as the body of rules, norms, and institutions that govern relations among the key players in the international environment. An order is a stable, structured pattern of relationships among states that involves some combination of parts, including emergent norms, rulemaking institutions, and international political organizations or regimes, among others.The distinguishing characteristic of an order is this settled, structured character: An order is distinguished from chaos, or random relationships, by some degree of pattern and structure. Ikenberry (2001, p. 23) similarly defines an order as a set of ‘governing arrangements between states, including its fundamental rules, principles, and institutions.’”

（在本研究中，我们将 “秩序” 定义为**规范国际社会核心行为体间关系的规则、规范与机构的集合**。秩序是国家间稳定、结构化的关系模式，包含新兴规范、规则制定机构、国际政治组织或机制等要素的组合。秩序的显著特征在于其 “稳定的结构化属性”：秩序与混乱或随机关系的区别，在于它具备一定程度的模式与结构。伊肯伯里（2001，第 23 页）也将秩序定义为 “国家间的治理安排，包括基本规则、原则与机构”。）
