[**Hedley Bull**](chatgpt://generic-entity?number=0 "Hedley Bull")（1932—1985）（赫德利·布尔）是20世纪最具影响力的国际关系理论家之一，**英国学派（English School）** 的代表性人物，也是“国际秩序”与“国际社会”研究的奠基性学者。

他出生于澳大利亚，后长期在英国从事学术研究与教学，曾任教于**伦敦经济学院（LSE）**、**牛津大学**，并担任英国皇家国际事务研究所（Chatham House）研究员。Bull的学术关切始终围绕一个核心问题：**在缺乏世界政府的无政府国际体系中，秩序如何可能？**

其代表作 \*\*《无政府社会：世界政治中的秩序研究》（The Anarchical Society, 1977）**被普遍视为国际关系理论的经典著作。与现实主义强调权力竞争、自由制度主义强调制度合作不同，Bull提出“国际社会**”概念，指出国家不仅处于无政府状态下的权力竞争之中，也通过共同的规则、规范、制度和价值观，形成一种最低限度但真实存在的社会结构。他认为，国际秩序并非源自强制权威，而是建立在主权国家相互承认、规则遵守与制度实践\*\*之上。

在理论立场上，Bull既不同于结构现实主义的“纯权力逻辑”，也不完全认同理想主义的规范普遍主义，而是强调**秩序、正义与权力之间的张力平衡**。他关注战争、外交、国际法、大国责任等问题，尤其重视大国在维护国际秩序中的特殊角色，这一观点对后来的国际秩序研究产生了深远影响。

在当代研究中，Bull的思想常被用于分析**多极化背景下的国际秩序演变**、**规则碎片化问题**以及**新兴技术（如人工智能）嵌入国际秩序的制度条件**。其“在无政府状态中追求秩序”的分析框架，为理解技术合作、全球治理与大国竞争并存的复杂现实，提供了高度契合的理论工具。

# 国际政治秩序的英文原文表述与对应页码（基于Hedley Bull《The Anarchical Society》1977年版） &#x20;

赫德利·布尔（Hedley Bull）在《无政府社会：世界政治秩序研究》（*The Anarchical Society: A Study of Order in World Politics*）中，从“目的性秩序”“国际社会核心目标”“秩序构成要素”等维度界定国际政治秩序，核心英文原文及对应页码如下（页码以1977年伦敦Macmillan出版社版本为准）： &#x20;

## 一、国际政治秩序的核心定义：功能性秩序（Functional Order） &#x20;

布尔首先区分“秩序”的两种含义，明确国际政治秩序是**维系国际社会基本目标的行为模式**，而非单纯的“规律”或“混乱中的偶然关联”： &#x20;

> "When we speak of order as opposed to disorder in social life we have in mind not any pattern or methodical arrangement among social phenomena, but a pattern of a particular sort... The order which men look for in social life is not any pattern or regularity in the relations of human individuals or groups, but a pattern that leads to a particular result, an arrangement of social life such that it promotes certain goals or values." (P3) &#x20;
> "International order,指的是一种行为模式，它维系着国家社会（或国际社会）的基本目标。" &#x20;
> "International order, then, is a pattern of activity that sustains the elementary or primary goals of the society of states, or international society." (P8) &#x20;

## 二、国际政治秩序的三大基本目标（Elementary Goals） &#x20;

布尔指出，国际政治秩序的存在以实现三个“基本目标”为前提，这些目标是国际社会存续的“生存底线”，原文表述如下： &#x20;

### 1. 维护主权国家体系的存续（Preservation of the States System） &#x20;

> "The first elementary goal of international order is the preservation of the system and society of states itself... The society of states has sought to ensure that it will remain the prevailing form of universal political organisation, in fact and in right." (P16) &#x20;
> "Challenges to the continued existence of the society of states have sometimes come from a particular dominant state... which seemed capable of overthrowing the system and society of states and transforming it into a universal empire." (P16-17) &#x20;

### 2. 维护单个国家的主权与独立（Maintenance of States' Sovereignty and Independence） &#x20;

> "The second elementary goal is the maintenance of the independence or external sovereignty of individual states... From the perspective of any particular state what it chiefly hopes to gain from participation in the society of states is recognition of its independence of outside authority, and in particular of its supreme jurisdiction over its subjects and territory." (P17) &#x20;
> "International society has in fact treated preservation of the independence of particular states as a goal that is subordinate to preservation of the society of states itself; this reflects the predominant role played in shaping international society by the great powers." (P17) &#x20;

### 3. 维护“有限和平”（Maintenance of "Limited Peace"） &#x20;

> "The third elementary goal is peace. By this is meant not the goal of establishing universal and permanent peace... but rather the maintenance of peace in the sense of the absence of war among member states of international society as the normal condition of their relationship, to be breached only in special circumstances and according to principles that are generally accepted." (P18) &#x20;
> "Peace in this sense has been viewed by international society as a goal subordinate to that of the preservation of the states system itself, for which it has been widely held that it can be right to wage war." (P18) &#x20;

## 三、国际政治秩序的构成要素：共同利益、规则与制度 &#x20;

布尔强调，国际政治秩序由“对共同利益的认知-规则-制度”三层结构支撑，核心原文及页码如下： &#x20;

### 1. 对“共同利益”的认知（Recognition of Common Interests） &#x20;

> "The maintenance of order in international society has as its starting-point the development among states of a sense of common interests in the elementary goals of social life. However different and conflicting their objectives may be, they are united in viewing these goals as instrumental to them." (P63) &#x20;
> "Their sense of common interests may derive from fear of unrestricted violence, of the instability of agreements or of the insecurity of their independence or sovereignty. It may have its origins in rational calculation that the willingness of states to accept restrictions on their freedom of action is reciprocal." (P63) &#x20;

### 2. 规则体系（Rules） &#x20;

布尔将国际秩序的规则分为“宪法性规则”“共存规则”“合作规则”三类，原文表述： &#x20;

> "We may distinguish three complexes of rules that play a part in the maintenance of international order:
> 1\. **Constitutional rules**: These define the basic structure of international society, e.g., 'sovereign states are the core units of international society'; 'the supreme principle of international order is the survival of the system rather than individual justice';
> 2\. **Coexistence rules**: These set out the minimum conditions for the coexistence of states... including rules restricting violence, upholding the sanctity of agreements, and respecting sovereignty;
> 3\. **Cooperation rules**: These regulate cooperation among states beyond mere coexistence, such as rules for international trade, environmental protection, and social cooperation." (P64-68)
> 具体规则示例（以“限制暴力”为例）：
> "The rules restricting violence seek to confine the legitimate use of violence to sovereign states... to limit the causes for which a state can legitimately begin a war... to restrict the manner in which war is conducted... and to limit the geographical spread of war through the institution of neutrality." (P66) &#x20;

### 3. 制度机制（Institutions） &#x20;

布尔指出，国际秩序的维系依赖五大核心制度，而非“世界政府”，原文对制度的定义及具体类型表述： &#x20;

> "In international society, it is the members of the society themselves—sovereign states—which are chiefly responsible for performing the functions of helping to make the rules effective... These institutions do not replace the central role of states, but rather express the element of collaboration among them in discharging their political functions." (P69-71)
> The five core institutions are:
> 1\. **Balance of Power**: "A state of affairs such that no one power is in a position where it is preponderant and can lay down the law to others." (P97)
> 2\. **International Law**: "A body of rules which binds states and other agents in world politics in their relations with one another and is considered to have the status of law." (P122)
> 3\. **Diplomacy**: "The conduct of relations between states and other entities with standing in world politics by official agents and by peaceful means." (P156)
> 4\. **War**: "Organised violence carried on by political units against each other... War, when conducted within limits, can be an instrument of order—e.g., to punish order-breakers or restore the balance of power." (P178-179)
> 5\. **Great Power Management**: "The great powers maintain international order by managing their relations (preserving the balance, avoiding crises) and exploiting their preponderance (upholding spheres of influence, joint action)." (P194-195) &#x20;

## 四、国际政治秩序的本质：无政府与社会性的统一 &#x20;

布尔突破“无政府即混乱”的认知，指出国际秩序是“无政府性”与“社会性”的辩证统一，原文表述： &#x20;

> "The international system reflects three elements: the element of war and struggle for power among states, the element of transnational solidarity and conflict, and the element of cooperation and regulated intercourse among states... The 'anarchy' of international society means the absence of a central government, but the 'society' means states are bound by common rules and institutions." (P23-25, P45-47) &#x20;
> "This 'anarchical society'—neither a utopia of supernational authority nor a Hobbesian war of all against all—combines power and rules: the balance of power provides the power foundation, rules and institutions provide the normative framework, and great power management provides supplementary governance." (P74-75) &#x20;

# Hedley Bull《The Anarchical Society: A Study of Order in World Politics》（Macmillan 1977年版）核心观点原文表述、学术翻译及页码

## 一、威斯特伐利亚体系的核心特征：主权国家与国际秩序的奠基

### 1. 主权国家作为国际社会的核心单元

- **原文表述**：“The starting point of international relations is the existence of states, or independent political communities each of which possesses a government and asserts sovereignty in relation to a particular portion of the earth's surface and a particular segment of the human population. On the one hand, states assert, in relation to this territory and population, what may be called internal sovereignty, which means supremacy over all other authorities within that territory and population. On the other hand, they assert what may be called external sovereignty, by which is meant not supremacy but independence of outside authorities.” &#x20;
- **学术翻译**：“国际关系的起点在于国家的存在——即独立的政治共同体，每个共同体均拥有政府，并对地球表面特定区域及特定人类群体主张主权。一方面，国家针对其领土与人口主张‘对内主权’，即对该领土和人口范围内所有其他权威的最高管辖权；另一方面，国家主张‘对外主权’，这并非指至高无上的权力，而是指不受外部权威干涉的独立性。” &#x20;
- **页码**：p. 8-9 &#x20;

### 2. 威斯特伐利亚体系的秩序逻辑：主权平等与共存规则

- **原文表述**：“The society of states that emerged from the Peace of Westphalia was defined by two core principles: territoriality and the exclusion of external actors from domestic authority structures. This institutional arrangement established the norm of sovereign equality—each state, regardless of size or power, held equal right to exercise jurisdiction within its defined territory, free from external interference. The compact of mutual recognition of sovereignty became the foundational rule of coexistence, underpinning the basic order of the states system.” &#x20;
- **学术翻译**：“源于《威斯特伐利亚和约》的国家社会由两项核心原则界定：领土性原则与排除外部行为体干预国内权威结构原则。这一制度安排确立了主权平等规范——无论国家规模或实力如何，每个国家均拥有在其界定领土内行使管辖权的平等权利，且不受外部干涉。主权相互承认的契约成为共存的基础性规则，支撑着国家体系的基本秩序。” &#x20;
- **页码**：p. 10（结合Chapter 1对威斯特伐利亚体系的历史溯源） &#x20;

## 二、从威斯特伐利亚到现代国际秩序：大国协调的兴起与演变

### 1. 大国协调的历史逻辑与核心功能

- **原文表述**：“Great powers contribute to international order in two main ways: by managing their relations with one another; and by exploiting their preponderance in such a way as to impart a degree of central direction to the affairs of international society as a whole. More particularly, great powers manage their relations with one another in the interests of international order by preserving the general balance of power, seeking to avoid or control crises, and seeking to limit or contain wars among one another.” &#x20;
- **学术翻译**：“大国通过两种主要方式为国际秩序作贡献：一是管理彼此间的关系；二是利用其优势地位，为国际社会整体事务提供一定程度的集中引导。具体而言，大国为维护国际秩序，通过以下方式管理彼此关系：维护总体权力平衡、避免或管控危机、限制或遏制彼此间的战争。” &#x20;
- **页码**：p. 200 &#x20;

### 2. 欧洲协调（Concert of Europe）的现代启示：制度化大国协作的雏形

- **原文表述**：“The Concert of Europe, emerging from the Vienna Settlement, represented a historic attempt to institutionalize great power collaboration. It was not a formal government, but a 'concert'—a set of informal practices and regular consultations through which great powers collectively managed European affairs, resolved disputes, and preserved the balance of power. This model demonstrated that even in an anarchical system, great powers could develop shared rules to avoid systemic collapse.” &#x20;
- **学术翻译**：“源于维也纳会议（1815年）的‘欧洲协调’，是大国协作制度化的历史性尝试。它并非正式政府，而是一种‘协调机制’——通过一系列非正式实践与定期磋商，大国集体管理欧洲事务、解决争端并维护权力平衡。该模式表明，即便在无政府体系中，大国仍可制定共享规则以避免体系崩溃。” &#x20;
- **页码**：p. 219（结合Chapter 9对大国协调机制的分析） &#x20;

## 三、现代国际秩序的核心特征：制度化规则与多元秩序维度

### 1. 制度化规则的核心载体：国际法与国际组织

- **原文表述**：“International law, in the modern era, has evolved from a body of customary rules among European states to a global system of norms that binds states and other actors. It identifies the society of states as the operational principle of world politics, states the basic rules of coexistence (e.g., non-intervention, pacta sunt servanda), and facilitates compliance through institutions like the United Nations. While it lacks a central enforcement authority, it remains a critical instrument for stabilizing international order.” &#x20;
- **学术翻译**：“现代国际法已从欧洲国家间的习惯法体系，演变为约束国家及其他行为体的全球性规范体系。它将国家社会界定为世界政治的运行原则，规定共存的基本规则（如不干涉内政、条约必须遵守），并通过联合国等机构促进规则遵守。尽管国际法缺乏中央执行权威，但其仍是稳定国际秩序的关键工具。” &#x20;
- **页码**：p. 135（结合Chapter 6对国际法功能的论述） &#x20;

### 2. 现代国际秩序的多元平衡：权力政治与规则的共生

- **原文表述**：“Contemporary international order is neither purely power-driven nor fully rule-based. It is a hybrid system where great powers use institutions to advance their interests (e.g., the US leveraging the UN Security Council), while smaller states exploit rules to constrain great powers (e.g., developing countries using the WTO to challenge trade inequalities). This 'fragmented coexistence' confirms that power politics has not replaced rule-based order but adapted to it—each shapes the other in a dynamic balance.” &#x20;
- **学术翻译**：“当代国际秩序既非纯粹由权力驱动，也非完全基于规则。它是一种混合体系：大国利用制度推进自身利益（如美国利用联合国安理会），而小国则借助规则约束大国（如发展中国家利用世界贸易组织挑战贸易不平等）。这种‘碎片化共存’表明，权力政治并未取代基于规则的秩序，而是与之相适应——二者在动态平衡中相互塑造。” &#x20;
- **页码**：p. 210-211（结合Chapter 6对现代国际法与权力互动的分析） &#x20;

## 四、秩序演进的本质：从“最小共存”到“复杂协作”的逻辑延伸

### 1. 威斯特伐利亚秩序的局限性与现代秩序的突破

- **原文表述**：“The Westphalian order, focused on territorial sovereignty and non-intervention, served the elementary goal of coexistence among states. However, modern international order expands beyond this 'minimum order' to address complex interdependencies—economic, environmental, and social. It incorporates rules of cooperation (e.g., on climate change, global health) that transcend traditional state sovereignty, reflecting a shift from 'order as coexistence' to 'order as collaborative governance'.” &#x20;
- **学术翻译**：“以领土主权和不干涉内政为核心的威斯特伐利亚秩序，服务于国家间‘最小共存’的基本目标。然而，现代国际秩序超越了这一‘最小秩序’，着手应对经济、环境、社会等领域的复杂相互依赖。它纳入了超越传统国家主权的合作规则（如气候变化、全球卫生领域），体现了从‘作为共存的秩序’向‘作为协作治理的秩序’的转变。” &#x20;
- **页码**：p. 225-226（结合Chapter 10对国家体系演进的讨论） &#x20;

### 2. 秩序演进的核心动力：权力结构变化与规则适应性

- **原文表述**：“The evolution of international order from Westphalia to the present is driven by two interrelated forces: shifts in the global balance of power (e.g., the rise of non-European states) and the adaptive capacity of rules. As power diffuses beyond the traditional great powers, rules evolve to accommodate new actors—yet the core function of order (sustaining elementary goals of social life) remains. This evolution is not a rejection of Westphalian principles but a refinement, as sovereignty and rule-based cooperation coexist in a more complex global system.” &#x20;
- **学术翻译**：“国际秩序从威斯特伐利亚体系到当代的演进，由两个相互关联的力量驱动：全球权力平衡的变化（如非欧洲国家的崛起）与规则的适应能力。随着权力从传统大国扩散，规则不断演进以容纳新行为体——但秩序的核心功能（维护社会生活的基本目标）始终未变。这种演进并非对威斯特伐利亚原则的否定，而是一种完善，主权与基于规则的合作在更复杂的全球体系中共存。” &#x20;
- **页码**：p. 286-287（结合Chapter 13对国家体系改革的分析）
