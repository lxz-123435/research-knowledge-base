    Ikenberry G J.Liberal Leviathan: The Origins, Crisis, and Transformation of the American World Order［M］. Princeton: Princeton University Press, 2011.¶

    Ikenberry G J.Liberal Leviathan：The Origins, Crisis, and Transformation of the American World Order［M］. Princeton：Princeton University Press, 2011.

    G. John Ikenberry（约翰·伊肯伯里）是当代国际关系理论领域最具影响力的学者之一，美国著名政治学家，自由制度主义（liberal institutionalism）与国际秩序研究的代表人物。他长期致力于研究国际秩序的形成、演化与危机，尤其关注权力政治如何通过制度、规则与规范被“嵌入”“驯化” 这一核心问题。

    一、学术背景与身份¶

    Ikenberry 现为美国普林斯顿大学（Princeton University） 政治学与国际事务教授，长期在国际关系顶级期刊发表研究成果，曾担任《International Organization》等重要学术期刊的编辑或编委。他既是严肃的学术理论家，也是政策圈高度重视的思想型学者。

    二、核心理论贡献¶

    Ikenberry 最重要的理论贡献在于提出并系统阐释了 “自由国际秩序”（liberal international order） 的制度逻辑，其核心观点包括：

    1. 权力并非只能通过强制行使

    他认为，霸权国家（尤其是二战后的美国）并不只是通过军事与经济实力维持秩序，而是通过规则、制度与承诺机制，将自身权力“制度化”，从而降低他国的不确定性和抵抗意愿。 2. 国际秩序是“权力+规则”的复合结构

    在他看来，国际秩序并非现实主义所说的单纯权力均衡体系，也并非理想主义的纯规则共同体，而是权力政治与制度化规则并存、相互嵌套的结构。 3. 制度具有“约束强者”的功能

    Ikenberry 特别强调，大国主动接受规则约束，是其获得长期领导合法性与秩序稳定性的关键，这一点构成对传统现实主义的重要修正。

    三、代表性著作与学术地位¶

    其代表作 《Liberal Leviathan》（2011） 被广泛视为理解21世纪国际秩序转型、美国主导秩序危机以及多极化背景下制度韧性的经典著作。该书系统分析了：

    • 战后国际秩序的制度基础
    • 冷战后自由秩序的扩展
    • 新兴大国崛起与秩序不确定性
    • 权力政治回归与制度仍然存续的并存状态

    在当代学术版图中，Ikenberry 通常被归为自由制度主义阵营，但其理论明显吸收并回应了现实主义的权力逻辑，因此常被评价为 “最能与现实主义对话的制度主义者”。

    四、与你当前写作的直接关联¶

    在你的文本中，Ikenberry 的理论非常适合用来支撑以下判断：

    • “权力政治回归，但并未取代制度化规则”
    • “国际政治秩序呈现出竞争加剧与制度仍在并存的张力状态”
    • “即便在战略竞争背景下，国际制度仍能通过非强制性、间接性机制塑造国家行为”

    《自由主义利维坦：美利坚世界秩序的起源、危机与转型》主要观点原文表述及页码（基于 Princeton University Press 2011 年版） ¶

    一、自由主义霸权秩序的核心逻辑与战后建构 ¶

    1. 秩序本质：兼具等级性与自由主义特征的霸权体系 ¶

    • 原文表述：“The result was a particular type of liberal international order—a liberal hegemonic order. The United States took on the duties of building and running an international order, organizing it around multilateral institutions, alliances, special relationships, and client states. It was a hierarchical political order with liberal characteristics. Defined in terms of the provision of security, wealth creation, and social advancement, this liberal hegemonic order has been, arguably at least, the most successful order in world history.”
    • 页码：Preface, xi

    2. 战后秩序构建的双重动力：冷战对抗与自由主义秩序愿景 ¶

    • 原文表述：“The American-led liberal hegemonic order was actually the fusing of two order-building projects. One was driven by the unfolding Cold War struggle with the Soviet Union, organized around deterrence, containment, alliances, and the bipolar balance of power. The other was aimed at creating an open, stable, and managed order among the Western democracies and was conceived by American officials before the onset of the Cold War—at least as early as the issuance of the Atlantic Charter in 1941—drawing on and updating liberal internationalist ideas.”
    • 页码：Chapter 5, 161

    3. 制度约束的关键作用：权力与规则的共生 ¶

    • 原文表述：“Power and rules are not enemies; they can be friends, and they are both necessary in the production of liberal order. The United States has been one of the most successful order-building states in world history because it has combined the exercise of its power with the championing of rule-based order.”
    • 页码：Preface, xv

    二、秩序的危机：权威争议与体系转型压力 ¶

    1. 危机本质：权威分配的争议而非自由主义原则的崩塌 ¶

    • 原文表述：“I argue that the crisis that besets America-led liberal world order is a crisis of authority. A political struggle or contest has been ignited over the distribution of roles, rights, and authority within liberal international order. The hegemonic aspect of liberal order—that is, America’s role and the old hegemonic bargains that surround it—is under pressure. But the deeper logic of open and loosely rule-based international order remains widely embraced.”
    • 页码：Preface, xii

    2. 单极体系对秩序的冲击：美国权力优势与制度约束的张力 ¶

    • 原文表述：“Under conditions of unipolarity, the United States has incentives to move toward a hub-and-spoke system. However, to the extent that the leading state calculates that its unipolar power is waning or is rendered less effective in securing control over its environment because of a loss of legitimacy and the acquiescence of weaker states, it will find incentives to remain tied to other states through multilateral rules and institutions.”
    • 页码：Chapter 4, 120

    3. 小布什政府的单边主义对秩序的破坏 ¶

    • 原文表述：“The Bush administration sought to build on the transformations of the global system—the rise of unipolarity and the flipping of the Westphalian order—and articulate a new vision of American-centered order. Fundamentally, the Bush administration offered up a vision of order that was, in important respects, hegemony with imperial characteristics. The United States was to step forward and provide rule and order based on its unilateral assertion of power and rights. It is a vision of America as a conservative Leviathan. This post-Westphalian logic of order has failed. The world has rejected it, and the United States cannot sustain it.”
    • 页码：Chapter 6, 225

    三、秩序的未来：转型路径与美国的战略选择 ¶

    1. 秩序转型方向：更具包容性、非等级化的合作体系 ¶

    • 原文表述：“These dilemmas and problems that have made the U.S.-led liberal hegemonic order contested and unstable are not destroying liberal international order, but pushing and pulling states toward a new kind of liberal international order—more inclusive, less hierarchical, and infused with more complex forms of cooperation.”
    • 页码：Preface, xiii

    2. 美国的核心战略：通过规则引领与霸权契约重构 ¶

    • 原文表述：“I argue that the United States should ‘lead through rules’ and look for ways to renegotiate hegemonic bargains with other states. I also argue that the United States needs to recapture the public philosophy of liberal internationalism—a blend of liberal and realist thinking—that served it so well in the postwar decades.”
    • 页码：Preface, xiv

    3. 自由主义秩序的韧性：无替代方案与扩展逻辑 ¶

    • 原文表述：“There is simply no credible alternative to an open, rule-based international order... The contemporary crisis is only an argument about the allocation of responsibilities and power within liberal institutions, but they aren’t going to be thrown out... Liberal international order—hegemonic or otherwise—tends to be unusually integrative. It is an order that is easy to join and hard to overturn.”
    • 页码：Chapter 7, 279-280

    四、关键概念界定与理论框架 ¶

    1. 自由主义霸权（Liberal Hegemony）与帝国（Empire）的区分 ¶

    • 原文表述：“Liberal hegemony is hierarchical order built around political bargains, diffuse reciprocity, provision of public goods, and mutually agreeable institutions and working relationships... In contrast, empire is a hierarchical political system in which the dominant state exercises direct or indirect sovereign control over the decisions of subordinate states... The United States built hierarchical relations but also mutually agreed-upon rules and institutions. There are both command-based and consent-based logics embedded in the postwar American-led order.”
    • 页码：Chapter 2, 69-70

    2. 威斯特伐利亚体系的转型：主权规范弱化与单极权力崛起 ¶

    • 原文表述：“Transformations in the Westphalian system—the rise of unipolarity, eroded norms of state sovereignty, the shifting sources of violence, and the intensification of security interdependence—all make the management of liberal hegemony more difficult and problematic... The end of the Cold War ushered in a world system characterized by unipolarity and globalization. Relations between poles and peripheries shifted. During the Cold War, the liberal order was built primarily within the Western advanced industrial world. It existed within one half of the larger bipolar global system. With the collapse of the Soviet Union and the end of bipolarity, the ‘inside’ Western system became the ‘outside’ order.”
    • 页码：Preface, xiii; Chapter 6, 223

    2. 单极体系对规则约束的削弱 ¶

    • 原文表述：“Under conditions of unipolarity, the United States has incentives to move toward a hub-and-spoke system.     • 页码：Chapter 4, 120

    二、规则与制度冲击的具体表现：权力失衡与单边主义破坏 ¶

    1. 威斯特伐利亚体系转型对规则基础的侵蚀 ¶

    • 原文表述：“Transformations in the Westphalian system—the rise of unipolarity, eroded norms of state sovereignty, the shifting sources of violence, and the intensification of security interdependence—all make the management of liberal hegemony more difficult and problematic... The end of the Cold War ushered in a world system characterized by unipolarity and globalization. Relations between poles and peripheries shifted. During the Cold War, the liberal order was built primarily within the Western advanced industrial world. It existed within one half of the larger bipolar global system. With the collapse of the Soviet Union and the end of bipolarity, the ‘inside’ Western system became the ‘outside’ order.”
    • 学术翻译：“威斯特伐利亚体系的转型——单极格局的兴起、国家主权规范的弱化、暴力来源的变迁以及安全 interdependence（相互依赖）的加剧——所有这些都使自由主义霸权的治理变得更加困难且充满问题……冷战的终结催生了一个以单极与全球化为特征的世界体系。中心与边缘的关系发生转变。冷战时期，自由主义秩序主要构建于西方发达工业世界内部，是更大规模双极全球体系中的‘半边天’。随着苏联解体与双极格局终结，西方‘内部’体系转变为全球‘外部’秩序。”
    • 页码：Preface, xiii; Chapter 6, 223

    2. 小布什政府单边主义对规则体系的破坏 ¶

    • 原文表述：“The Bush administration sought to build on the transformations of the global system—the rise of unipolarity and the flipping of the Westphalian order—and articulate a new vision of American-centered order. Fundamentally, the Bush administration offered up a vision of order that was, in important respects, hegemony with imperial characteristics. The United States was to step forward and provide rule and order based on its unilateral assertion of power and rights. It is a vision of America as a conservative Leviathan. This post-Westphalian logic of order has failed. The world has rejected it, and the United States cannot sustain it.”
    • 学术翻译：“小布什政府试图借助全球体系的转型——单极格局的兴起与威斯特伐利亚秩序的倒置——构建以美国为中心的新秩序愿景。从本质上看，小布什政府提出的秩序愿景，在关键方面具有‘带帝国特征的霸权’属性。美国本应凭借单边权力与权利主张，主动承担起构建规则与秩序的责任。这是将美国定位为‘保守型利维坦’的愿景。这种后威斯特伐利亚秩序逻辑已然失败：世界各国拒绝接受，美国也无法维系。”
    • 页码：Chapter 6, 225

    三、规则与制度冲击的根源：权力转移与合法性赤字 ¶

    1. 权力结构变化对规则共识的挑战 ¶

    • 原文表述：“These dilemmas and problems that have made the U.S.-led liberal hegemonic order contested and unstable are not destroying liberal international order, but pushing and pulling states toward a new kind of liberal international order—more inclusive, less hierarchical, and infused with more complex forms of cooperation.”
    • 学术翻译：“那些导致美国主导的自由主义霸权秩序陷入争议与不稳定的困境和问题，并非在摧毁自由主义国际秩序，而是在推动各国朝着一种新型自由主义国际秩序演进——这种新秩序更具包容性、层级性更弱，并融入了更为复杂的合作形式。”
    • 页码：Preface, xiii

    2. 霸权合法性下降与规则约束力弱化 ¶

    • 原文表述：“Power and rules are not enemies; they can be friends, and they are both necessary in the production of liberal order. The United States has been one of the most successful order-building states in world history because it has combined the exercise of its power with the championing of rule-based order. But when the leading state’s authority is no longer securely anchored in shared bargains or legitimate institutions, the rules themselves become contested.”
    • 学术翻译：“权力与规则并非对立关系，二者可互为助力，且在构建自由主义秩序的过程中均不可或缺。美国之所以能成为世界历史上最成功的秩序构建国之一，正是因为它将权力行使与倡导以规则为基础的秩序相结合。但当主导国的权威不再稳固地植根于共同契约或合法制度时，规则本身便会陷入争议。”
    • 页码：Chapter 7, 279-280

    3. 新兴力量崛起对旧规则分配的冲击 ¶

    • 原文表述：“The global liberal system has outgrown its American-led, hegemonic foundation. This is a problem—but it is a problem of success rather than failure of the liberal project... The rise of new power centers will come with new agendas for organizing the basic logic and principles of international order. If these new centers reject the old rules, the liberal order’s institutional framework will face unprecedented strain.”
    • 学术翻译：“全球自由主义体系已超越了以美国为主导的霸权基础。这固然是一个问题，但它是自由主义项目‘成功带来的问题’，而非失败所致……新兴权力中心的崛起，必然会带来重构国际秩序基本逻辑与原则的新议程。若这些新兴中心拒绝接受旧规则，自由主义秩序的制度框架将面临前所未有的压力。”
    • 页码：Chapter 7, 285-286

    四、规则与制度的韧性：无替代方案与改革空间 ¶

    1. 自由主义规则的不可替代性 ¶

    • 原文表述：“There is simply no credible alternative to an open, rule-based international order... The contemporary crisis is only an argument about the allocation of responsibilities and power within liberal institutions, but they aren’t going to be thrown out... Liberal international order—hegemonic or otherwise—tends to be unusually integrative. It is an order that is easy to join and hard to overturn.”
    • 学术翻译：“目前，对于开放且以规则为基础的国际秩序而言，根本不存在可信的替代方案……当下的危机仅是围绕自由主义制度内部责任与权力分配的争议，而非要摒弃这些制度本身……无论是霸权型还是其他类型的自由主义国际秩序，都具有非凡的整合能力。这是一种‘易加入、难颠覆’的秩序。”
    • 页码：Chapter 7, 279-280

    2. 规则体系的改革路径：从霸权主导到多元共治 ¶

    • 原文表述：“I argue that the United States should ‘lead through rules’ and look for ways to renegotiate hegemonic bargains with other states. I also argue that the United States needs to recapture the public philosophy of liberal internationalism—a blend of liberal and realist thinking—that served it so well in the postwar decades.”
    • 学术翻译：“笔者认为，美国应‘通过规则引领’，并寻求与其他国家重新谈判霸权契约的途径。同时，美国需重拾自由主义国际主义的公共理念——这种理念融合了自由主义与现实主义思想，在战后数十年间为美国提供了有力支撑。”
